    Method

    Estimates come from the CDC PLACES project, which uses a multilevel-regression-and-poststratification model on Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System responses to produce small-area prevalence estimates for every U.S. census tract.

    Tract polygons are drawn from U.S. Census TIGER/Line 2020 boundaries for Milwaukee County (FIPS 55079).

    The metric — frequent mental distress — is the share of adults age 18+ who report 14 or more days of poor mental health in the past month.

    Read the map carefully. Color shows estimated prevalence, not lived experience. Tracts with similar shading can have very different histories, demographics, and access to care.
